Sushant Singh Rajput

Sushang Singh Rajput (21 January 1986 – 14 June 2020) was an Indian actor who is best known for his work in Hindi cinema.[1] He has starred in a number of commercially successful Bollywood films such as M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story (2016), Kedarnath (2018) and Chhichhore (2019). Due his contribution to the film industry, he was the recipient of one Screen Award and was nominated for the Filmfare Awards on two occasions.

Rajput started his career after dropping out of his engineering course at the Delhi College of Engineering and entering the theatre industry in Mumbai. He moved on to feature in Hindi television serials, his debut show was the romantic drama Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil (2008), followed by the lead role in the soap opera Pavitra Rishta (2009–2011). He made his Bollywood debut in the film adaptation Kai Po Che! (2013) which became a commercial and critical success. Followed by his starring role in the romantic comedy Shuddh Desi Romance (2013) and the role of the titular detective in the action thriller Detective Byomkesh Bakshy! (2015).

He has also featured in the starring role in a number of other films such as the reincarnation drama Raabta (2017), the historical drama Sonchiriya (2019) and the action thriller Drive (2019). His highest-grossing releases came with a supporting role in the satire PK (2014), and from the titular role in the sports biopic of Mahendra Singh Dhoni. For his performance in the latter, he received his first nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Actor and became the winning nomination at the Indian Film Festival of Melbourne. Apart from acting, he was the founder of a non profit organisation and has been credited as the co-founder of a technological startup.

Rajput died by suicide at his home in Bandra, Mumbai in June 2020, aged 34.[2][3][4] His last film, Dil Bechara (2020), was released posthumously on the streaming platform Hotstar.

Early life and education

Sushant Singh Rajput was born in Patna in the state of Bihar to Krishna Kumar Singh and Usha Singh.[1][5][6] He was the youngest of five siblings and had the nickname of Gulshan.[7] One of his four sisters Mitu Singh would become a state-level cricket player.[5] He attended the St. Karen's High School in Patna.[7][8] His family moved to Delhi following his mother's death in 2002 where Rajput completed his schooling in Kulachi Hansraj Model School.[6].

Rajput was reportedly an avid reader who was deeply interested in astrophysics and won the National Olympiad in Physics.[9] He secured an admission in the Delhi College of Engineering (later renamed to Delhi Technological University) to pursue a Bachelor of Engineering degree in mechanical engineering.[10][9] According to Rajput, he did not have any interest in engineering but his family gave him no option which left him dissatisfied. He instead wanted to become an astronaut and later an air force pilot but was also interested in Bollywood, being a fan of Shah Rukh Khan.[11]

Career

2006–2011: Early career and television

During his course at the Delhi College of Engineering, Rajput enrolled himself in Shiamak Davar's dance classes.[5] Soon afterwards, he also began attending acting classes under the theatre director Barry John.[12][13] Rajput later stated that he found the experience to be liberating and wanted to continue doing it forever.[14] He featured as a background dancer in the Dhoom Again song of Dhoom 2 and in Ashwariya Rai's performance at the closing ceremony of the 2006 Commonwealth Games.[15] In 2006,[16] he dropped out of his engineering course during his fourth year to begin a career in arts and performance.[17] He moved to Mumbai, took up odd jobs and small roles while working in the theatre industry.[18] In Mumbai, Rajput joined Nadira Babbar's theatre group called the Ekjute in which he remained for two and a half years.[19][20]

Rajput was spotted by a casting team of Balaji Telefilms while he was working at the Prithvi Theatre.[12] He was subsequently called for an audition and cast as a second lead in the television series Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil in 2008.[21][22] He had secured the role of Preet Singh Juneja, the step brother of the lead named Harshad Chopda and entered the show in its seventh episode.[23] The character was killed early in the show, but due to popularity among viewers was brought back in the series finale as a spirit looking on as his family celebrated coming out of difficult times.[24] The performance had made an impression on the producer Ekta Kapoor who vouched for him to be cast as the lead in another series Pavitra Rishta,[21] reportedly against the wishes of the hosting channel, Zee TV.[25][26]

In Pavitra Rishta, Rajput was cast as Manav Deshmukh who was an "ideal son and husband" and co-starred along with Ankita Lokhande.[27] The role was reportedly a breakthrough for him and for which he won various television awards,[28] including his first award for best actor in the Indian Television Academy Awards 2010.[29] He also won three major television awards in the categories of best male actor and most popular actor.[30] In 2010, he joined the dance reality show Zara Nachke Dikha in its second season as part of the Mast Kalandar Boys team.[31][30] On the Mother's Day special episode, the team dedicated a performance to Rajput's mother who had died.[32] Later in the same year, he also took part in the fourth season of another dance competition reality show called Jhalak Dikhhla Jaa and was paired alongside the choreographer Shampa Sonthalia; they emerged as the runners up of the season.[31]

Rajput continued to act in Pavitra Rishta from its first airing on 2009 till his exit in 2011,[33] having played the part of the protagonist of the series for two and a half years at the time of his exit.[34] He had stated that he intended to move to the United States to study filmmaking and had already gained experience behind the scenes in the film Raaz 2,[22] where he was an assistant director.[35] Rajput made a later reappearance in the final episode of Pavitra Rishta in 2014 after gaining critical acclaim in Bollywood.[34]
